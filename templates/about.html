About PocketPinch

Why PocketPinch?

PocketPinch was created to provide it's users with a way to save money without being made to feel bad about their choices. PocketPinch was designed with the desire to allow it's users to tailor the money saving tips and tricks they'd like to adapt. These tips and tricks are targeted towards allowing it's users to reach a variety of money saving goals and implement various money saving strategies.

PocketPinch is important to us because financial literacy is typically a sensitive topic within families. This taboo nature surrounding talks of financial literacy has made it so that families in poverty often remain in poverty. By creating PocketPinch, we're hoping that we can help to normalize talks surrounding financial literacy and aid families in developing healthy money saving strategies. The hope is that users will soon realize saving money and pinching pennies doesn't have to be anything to be ashamed of.


Future of PocketPinch

More Money Personalities: While we are extremely proud of the progress we've made in PocketPinch, we acknowledge that we have a long way to go. Our current product design does a good job of providing users with the general sense of the type of spender they are and how to save money. In the future, we'd like to increase our number of money habit personalities. Increasing the personality count would allow us to better service a wide disaspera of individuals.

Linking Bank Accounts: Additionally, we'd like to allow our users to link their bank accounts. Providing this service will allow us to better analyze our users spending habits. With better data surrounding each user, we'd be able to create a more tailored and customizable plan for them to follow to reach their spending goals. We'd also be able to allow users to opt in/out of our various plans and spending tips.

Coupons: While analyzing our users spending habits, we intend to provide them with coupons to stores they frequent and items they frequently purchase. If users opt-in to geolocation, we'd notify our users if there's a coupon we think they'd like at the store their visitng.

Geolocation: Having the options of allowing our users to opt in to geolocation tracking would greatly optimize our product. This addition would allow us to track which restaurants and stores our users visit. In combination with our bank linking feature, we'd be able to analzye how often our users visit a store versus how often they spend money at that store. We'd be able to use this analysis to further customize our plans for our users. These customization would include allowing users to set a spending goal for a specific store; congratulating users if they reach that goal or providing unique tips for the user to follow if they aren't able to meet their goal.

Alternative Spending Suggestions: This would argueably be our most beneficial feature to our users. Alternative spending suggestions involves analyzing the specific items that our users buy. In this analysis, we'd be looking to see if our users could save money by purchasing a smaller size of a drink, or changing the clothing brand they buy from. If we're able to find good alternatives to our users' typical items, we'll suggest that the user switch the items that they purchase. Users would have the option to opt certain items out, if they prefer the quality and don't want to be given alternative suggestion.
Alternative spending suggestions also includes notifying our users if they should wait to purchase an item. Say a user is about to purchase an item that's going to go on sale in the next few days. The alternative suggestions feature would inform the user that the item will be going on sale soon, and suggest that they wait to purchase the item.
